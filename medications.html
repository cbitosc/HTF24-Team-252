Medications for Symptoms

Disorder Medications
Dysmenorrhea (Painful Periods) Pain relievers (NSAIDs like ibuprofen or naproxen) are commonly used to reduce pain. Birth control pills can help regulate hormones, which may reduce cramps
Amenorrhea (Absence of Periods) Hormone therapy (such as estrogen or progestin) may be prescribed if hormonal imbalances are the cause. Lifestyle changes, like managing stress or weight, may also help.
Menorrhagia (Heavy Periods) NSAIDs can reduce blood loss. Birth control pills or hormonal IUDs may be recommended to regulate bleeding. Tranexamic acid, an antifibrinolytic medication, can also help reduce bleeding.
Oligomenorrhea (Infrequent Periods) Hormonal birth control can help regulate cycles. Treating underlying conditions (like PCOS) may also improve frequency.
